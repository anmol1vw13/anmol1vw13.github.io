An easy and clear trek, Rayakottai is best for beginners. With a fort on the hilltop, it is one of the ancient fortresses in the Krishnagiri district. In the 18th century, Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan ruled this fort and is now one of the protected monuments of the Archaeological Survey of India.

Although it is just at a distance of 80 kms from Bangalore, the place isn't crowded at all. 
Thanks to the TamilNadu road tax, which is very high! 

Baseline
Crew members: Anmol, Triyansha, Rishabh
Start time: 7AM
Transportation: Traveller via BMC (Bangalore Mountaineering Club)

We were going to have a long weekend and since I was already on a trek fever, trekking was a natural choice. This time I began the planning on Wednesday. I went up to Ponkishore but he had other plans (home calling!). He suggested to try tagging along with a trekking group for a change and sent me a link to the Bangalore Mountaineering Club. 
The weekend fixed departure section had some really interesting places but since I wanted to start the long weekend off with a trek, I chose the one on Friday. 
After booking my ticket I randomly asked a colleague (Rishabh) and my cousin (Triyansha); she had recently moved to Bangalore. Dono maan gye!!! I booked their tickets as well and we were set for the long weekend.

Getting there

BMC has a lot of pickup points, SO IT'S QUITE CONVENIENT. 
7AM was the time given to us and Boy, they were punctual! 
However, within an hour's drive, they halted for breakfast at a Udupi restaurant, when we should have had started the trek by 8!

The service was super slow and it took 45mins for everyone to be done with their breakfast. We were a group of 21 people! We were also given our lunch packs (Pulav).
I dozed off the moment we got into the Traveller. After about an hour, I got up, hoping we were at the destination. But nooooo, we were on the service road, waiting for the driver and the organizers. 
My word of advice, do not travel to Tamil Nadu on the 1st of November. There was a heavy queue at the Tamil Nadu RTO for the road tax. 
We were scheduled to reach the spot by 9AM, but we reached at 11 instead. The sun was bright and the heat was unbearable.
Nevertheless, the instructor huddled us and briefed us about the fort and the guidelines for the trek.



We started trekking at around 11:15 AM. Rishabh and Triyansha, both of them kept panting along the way- probably because this was their first trek (ha! novices) or because they weren't fit enough :P

The terrain was very smooth and we had no problems going through.

                 
            




Dinosaurs making out eh? :P
It was a short trek and we reached the top by 12:30. It being quite breezy on the top, helped with the heat. We had, however, run out of water! (Do carry lots of water, 3 bottles each at least!).


We chose a spot with maximum shade and rested up for a while. Video-called our parents. It was quite a surprise for my Mom when she saw my cousin.

After lunch, we started our descent by 2PM. Since we had no water left, it was very tiring. We reached the bottom in an hour and immediately purchased a few bottles (water of course!)

The trek would have been fun if the organizers kept everyone involved. It didn't at all feel as though we were in a group. Maybe, that's just how BMC operates :)

We reached back, just-wanna-drop-dead kind of exhausted, and yet fulfilled, in ways, I can't find the words for.
