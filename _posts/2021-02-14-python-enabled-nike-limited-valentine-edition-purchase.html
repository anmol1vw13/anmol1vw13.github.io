Valentine's day was around the corner.

To make it memorable, I decided to get her something exclusive.

My sister then got to know from a friend of her's about a valentine edition Nike sneaker, which was going to be launched soon.

I loved the idea of gifting a limited edition shoe. Since I had never purchased one myself before, the excitement to bag it was obvious. The only thought I had was - A shoe with LOVE engraved!! CHEEEEEESY much? 🙉
But I shrug it off because it is after-all a valentine's day and it deserves such cheesiness. 😅

There was one problem though. Nike Limited Edition shoes have a reputation of going out of stock within hours of the launch. Hence timing the purchase was going to be extremely crucial.

It was 1st February and I was expecting the sneaker to be launched by 6th of Feb, since that was the date speculated by a lot of sneaker enthusiasts online.

Monitoring Nike continuously would have been time consuming and hence the first thing that struck me was - If only had a feature to notify about the launch 😓

Not being able to find any viable solution to this, I took it upon myself to make one.

Firstly, I figured out if there's any difference between the pages of products available right now and the products yet to be launched!

The visible difference is the text Coming Soon not being present in the products which are in stock.

In HTML terminology, this translates into a div with data-text="comingSoon"` not being present when product is in stock.

All I needed was a script which continuously scraped the page and check for the tag being present. The moment the product comes in stock, the tag would go away and in such case, a notification needed to be sent.

Cooked up a script written in python the very same day.

The script makes use of two external services

Call Me Bot for sending whatsapp messages.

Wirepusher for sending mobile notifications.

The links have detailed description on how to set up both of them.

This script now had to be run on a machine and had to be run on regular intervals i.e. a cron had to be setup.
I chose GCP to be the cloud platform. You could follow this article to get it done.
Once the machine was up, I cloned the repository.
The only thing left was to get the API Keys and set up a cron which ran every 2 minutes.

*/2 * * * * env MOBILE_NUMBER="#mobile_number" CALL_ME_BOT_KEY="#call_me_bot_key" WIRE_PUSHER_KEY="#wire_pusher_key" python /scripts-py/nikeshoe_alerter.py >> /log.py

All I now had to do was take a back seat and wait for the notification to arrive. 👻

Meanwhile, I got her home address from her sister and asked her to keep it a surprise. Not a single word! Sssshhhhhh 🤫

Saved the address on nike to be swift like a cheetah during the launch 😎

Having waited for 6+ days, we lost complete hope about the gift being delivered on time, even if it became available.

Nevertheless, when the notification finally came up on 8th of Feb, my happiness knew no bounds!! The adrenaline rush..

Making use of my programming skills to get this done felt like an achievement!! Sure, a little over the top, but it did make me proud 🙊.

The shoe was arriving from Singapore and was being delivered by Aramex estimated to reach between 12th and 24th 🤐. Insane amount of buffer right? Kher, Videsh se aa raha hai toh banta hai! 🤭

I went through the procedure to get a customs clearance for the shipment on 10th and to our luck, it got delivered right on time, on 14th morning.
The shoes were amazing and the stunning black box in which they came was even better.

She absolutely loved the sneakers, which made our tech enabled Valentine's Day, although virtual, a huge success!! 💛
