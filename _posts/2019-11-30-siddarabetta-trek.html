SiddaraBetta, located at about 100 km from Bangalore, is one of the most sought after destinations for a day trek. It is surrounded by natureâ€™s utmost blessings and is known for old temples and caves as well.

Baseline
Crew members: Anmol, Ponkishore, Arun, Rishabh, Devansh, Gaurang, Rohit, Devendra, Raushan
Time: 5AM
Transportation: Ola DriveSssss



I didn't ever think I would have so many people going with me on one of my treks. How this happened was via one of the tech townhalls and all the folks were asking about company-sponsored trips. The CTO casually pointed out about me going on treks every weekend and that I should take the team along some time.
So, that night I decided upon a place and put a formal email inviting everyone in the tech.
The response wasn't very positive. Only 5 people replied with a '+1'.
Since we were a total of 6 people, we hired, guess what, an Innova Crysta, hahaha!
On Friday, two more people showed interest (Devendra and Raushan). So, we booked another car. I don't know why but Arun booked a Swift Desire (Taxi Kaun Book Karta hai Bhai?ðŸ™…)
But then Ola Drive canceled our Crysta, at the last min due to unavailability and gave us a Hyundai Creta (a 5 seater). But since we had two cars, and we were 9 of us, it worked out okay.

Getting there
Both cars had different pickup spots. Desire at Forum mall, Kormangala, and Creta at Garuda mall, MG Road. Being the punctual group we are, we reached on time at 4AM and got our cars picked.
Swift, however, broke down the moment we got it out. They had to jump-start the car to get it to work.

Car to Car CPR!
By 5 AM, the car was ready and we then hit the road.
In about 2 hours after halting for tea, we were at the base. Parking was easy but going ahead was a challenge. There were a lot of hungry monkeys and some of them just wouldn't budge. Ponkishore had to throw away the biscuits he was carrying just to pacify them.

Arun aur uska bhai :P

The trail starts with an arch, and steps of rock slabs lying at the foot of the hill. After treading for a while, the trail becomes uneven, steps at places and at others, steep with railings. All along the way, there were ample seats and huts for us to catch our breath.






A big group, had us taking a lot of short breaks. Well, such halts gave us enough time to capture some of the beauty of the place!



With music all along and an hour's worth of walk, we reached a temple, which was presumed to be the top by a lot of folks in the group.
Dejected, a few of us started exploring around and finally found another trail. Yay!
The path was a bit risky. We had to go across huge boulders and crawl through rock crevices.



After about 30 mins of trudging, we reach the hilltop. At the summit, one could see the ruins of a fort and a Shiva temple as well.

 


The view and the breeze - breathtaking! It seemed as though the whole summit was a monolithic rock.
Being the only group present that day, it was quite peaceful!




A few minutes of stroll and a million pictures later, we took shelter at a cave-like enclosure to relax.
Carrying food is a must because this place has zero commercialization (which is positive)!!

Men at work :P

10 minutes of meditation with the breeze was absolute bliss. After all, it is the Hill of Saints!


After chilling for an hour and a half on the summit, we started our descent. It took us about an hour's time to reach the base. We drove back and had our lunch in Tumkur at a restaurant called The Hut.
After the wholesome lunch, we got back to Bangalore, dropped the cars and parted.

I couldn't have asked for a better way to mark the end of my 7-week streak. Siddarabetta no-doubt was the best of them all. The group, the adventure, the view, the hard trail - all equally contributed to an exhilarating Saturday!
