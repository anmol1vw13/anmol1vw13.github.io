Channagiri, also known as Channaragiri is the ideal trek, to begin with, if you're looking to kickstart your trekking escapades/journey.
A part of the Nandi Hills range, Channagiri lies at a distance of about 70kms from the Bangalore city.
It takes about 2 and a half hours to go uphill, owing to its distance of approximately 3kms from the start point.

Baseline
Crew members: Anmol, Arun, Ponkishore
Start time: 5AM
Transportation: Ola Outstation


It was just a casual Friday evening at the office when I had this sudden urge of doing something interesting for the weekend. And after much pondering, I decided to go on a trek. Little did I know that this was to be the stepping stone of my trek streak. I went around the office, asked a few of my colleagues only to face rejection until Ponkishore accepted the request. Arun overheard our conversation and decided to join us on our expedition(he didn't have anything better to do, anyway).

Now since it was my idea, I had to be in charge of location and transportation. After googling a bit, I pinned a spot and booked an Ola outstation.
The spot although wasn't Channagiri.
It was Uttari Betta instead.





Getting there

Ola outstation had arrived at my doorstep at 5AM. It took an hour to pick Ponkishore, Arun and
leave Bangalore. While on the way, I was going through multiple blogs on the Uttari Betta trek, sadly,
I didn't find it as exciting as I had expected it to be. Googled a bit and that's how we landed on Channagiri.

We reached the place at 7AM only to find a board saying
Entrance is prohibited; trespassers will be prosecuted.

Our reaction?





We went in, not giving two shits about the consequence.
The journey uphill now began. Not having been on a trek for long, we had to take a
lot of breaks during the first hour of the journey. The visibility was low. with mist and fog all around.




On our way up, we saw another group, mostly engrossed in clicking selfies. 
There were a lot of moments in which we had given up, either because we couldn't find the right path or because we couldn't see anything ahead ( much like life!). At one point in time, we were just getting through the thick bushes, hoping to not encounter a wild animal or a reptile.
We reached a point, we thought was the end of the trek. Spent 30mins of our time with music and pictures.

   

The mist then cleared up a bit, and we could see the "raasta"! Our faces lit up, as we still had grounds to cover and it wasn't an end to our trek. After much trudging, lugging and struggling, we finally reached our destination. 
Hurray!!!!
The view and the breeze, mashallah! 


Having reached the top by 9:30 AM, we had plenty of time to enjoy the scenic place.
What better way to spend time than to click pictures, right? Arun's leather jacket and goggles were put to good use.

  

There was also a temple on the top, which had a lake of its own- a miniature version of the type that is seen in Varanasi ( not very clean, though).  


After the photoshoot session and a few minutes of meditation, we started our descent. It surprisingly took us only an hour to get down. We probably treaded an easier path. 

Channagiri's Chandrayaan :P



We were then welcomed by an officer, who had greed in his eyes. He pointed his fingers towards the board and asked us to read it. With complete innocence, we told him that we were unaware of the board, blaming it on the mist, and we wouldn't have gone in had we seen the charges of trespassing. Looking for a quick escape, we handed him 200 Rs and fled from there.

Starving, we found our savior in the Indian Paratha Factory. 


The parathas were YUM!

 And just like that, with our bellies full, our bodies and us, ready to hit the sack, we bid ......bang.
