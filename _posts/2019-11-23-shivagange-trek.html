The place I had initially chosen was Bhairavi Durga. The trail of it is pretty steep and you've to have done a lot of trekking to tread such path. I changed the spot to Shivagange since most of them didn't have prior trekking experience.

Getting there

So, the plan was to gather at Sudhanv's place by 4:30 AM which was when we were scheduled to leave.
Waking up at 3:30, the first thing I saw was a message from Ponkishore saying he wouldn't be able to come because he had a long night at the office. I have to admit, my motivation came down too since I didn't sleep much either.
I called up Sudhanv next. He picked the call and said he was ready.
That got me charged up! Hell, yeah.

So, from 5 we were down to just the 2 of us but we still decided to go for it.
I reached Sudhanv's place at 4:50 and we hit the road by 5.


Driving a hatchback, after an SUV can be a bit tricky. The controls aren't very smooth.
I drove for a bit but then gave the wheels back to Sudhanv.

We reached by 6:30. It is a quiet village with lots of monkeys around.
The trek begins with an entry to the famous Girigangadhareshwara & Honnadevi Temple. The initial climb had about 200 steps. By the time we reached the temple, Sudhanv had realized he's gotta start working out. Hahaha.



We then met a man, who apparently, had been coming on the same trek for the past 18 days for spiritual reasons. He knew a great deal about the place but because he was speaking in Kannada I couldn't grasp much of it (Sudhanv however, found a good friend in him).

The man then stopped at a spot that had a statue of Lord Shiva's with little water around. He drank some of it, saying the water is holy and is believed to cure diseases.


With a lot of short breaks and an hour into the trek, we reached a small Ganesha temple, with idols of Shiva, Parvati, and Nandi.


The final ascent was quite tricky and challenging. Without a good pair of shoes, you might end up bruising yourself.





 
The steps were extremely steep, and after much trudging, we finally reached the top by 8:30. The breeze was better than all the places I had been to (Naa Kam, Naa Zyaada).

There's also a stall, in case you need to re-energize yourself. Fruit chat, Bhelpuri, Sevpuri, Chips, Buttermilk, Cucumber are few of the eatables, the stall had to offer.
I had a cucumber - super refreshing.

The view from the top was stunning and one could chill there all day.

Titanic?
The view!
Although a bit crowded, this place did have a few spots where one could find peace. I found one for myself and meditated for about 40 minutes.

The meditation spot.

All through the trek, we were accompanied by monkeys & they attacked anyone with eatables. Hence it is a good idea to trek the hills without any edible stuff in your hands.


We stayed on the top for about an hour and a half after which we started our descent. It felt like a stroll in the park and we covered the path in just an hour. So, it took us around 3 hours, to and fro and I felt like a pro (Aye, that rhymes!ðŸ˜‹).

The folks at the temple were having some sort of langar and were insisting all the trekkers to eat something before leaving. They had served tomato rice and a dessert which was made of rice. It was fillingðŸ˜…

We drove back to Bangalore. I had lunch at Sudhanv's place. Aunty had prepared PavBhaji, Gobi Ke Paranthe, and Rasagulla. 
The food was very tasty. After the hearty lunch and some much-needed rest, I got back to my place and slept like a baby!

I would rate the trek to be moderately difficult and would also recommend to visit it at least once.

Happy Trekking! :)
