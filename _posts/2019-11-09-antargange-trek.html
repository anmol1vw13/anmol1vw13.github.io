Antargange hill, a place known for its night treks, is one of the famous pilgrim destinations located just 4 km from Kolar town and 70 km from Bangalore.

Baseline
Crew members: Anmol, Ponkishore, Avinash, Ritesh
Start time: 5AM
Transportation: Ola Drive





This time we were a slightly bigger group, friends and colleagues which meant never-ending plans and of course, making of a Whatsapp group, allocating responsibilities and whatnot.
This was also going to be a one of a kind trip because I had a crazy idea of making tea, uphill (The Smoked Tea). 

The Samagri needed to make tea was divided amongst the 5 of us. Ritesh wasn't assigned anything.

Raw materials.

We had to book a MUV (Multi Utility Vehicle) since we were a big group and because we wanted to self drive this time around, Innova Crysta was chosen. Avinash, Rishabh, and Devansh were going to be our designated सारथी

Getting there

Forum Mall, Koramangala was where we had to pick the car from. Rishabh, despite suffering from viral, came to the spot, helped us in getting the car.
We waited for Devansh but he couldn't join us either. His mobile apparently was with his flatmate because of which he couldn't tend to the alarms (lame).
I now had to drive and pick Ponkishore, Avinash. Was a bit tricky but was smooth since no one died :P
From 6, we were reduced to 4 people and we had a huge ass car with us. Driving an Innova was fun!

We hit the road at 6AM. Avinash was at the wheel. Took us 1 and a half hour to cover the 70kms.

We parked the car at the base and started walking towards the temple.
With 350 stairs leading to the Temple, the path was lush green.




Once at the Temple, there are plenty of over-enthusiastic guides, for a guided tour if needed. 
We, however, decided against a guide, self-exploration, you see!

      
                  The trail


Viewpoint

















After a 45min trail, we reached the caves. We met another trekker from the UK who was traveling along with a guide. We had to follow him since he was right ahead of us.  Having someone to guide us turned out to be a boon since the route through the caves was extremely tricky. Going deep would have been impossible otherwise.



               
The caves were exciting and took us 45 mins to get through. The guide asked us to get back because there wasn't anything worthwhile to see next. We insisted to go further, paid him 100 bucks and asked him to go back.

After a 10 minute walk, we found a quiet place to do what we had come for - CHAI!!

Find the steps below in case you want to try making it yourself.

Step 1: Scout for dry sticks.
Step 2: Find a few sturdy rocks and arrange them to make a hearth.
Step 3: Put some camphor and light the fire using a matchstick or a lighter.
Step 4: Chai Banao xD


The ash came in handy to clean up the utensils.
PS: Please do not litter the place. We took all the garbage back with us :)

After gorging on the yum tea, we stayed for a while, gazing at the view and embracing the breeze.


      
      Kya Baat Hai!!
          ( We didn't write this )


      Chal Wahan Jaate Hain!















The descent took us about an hour. We had a lot of fun with the monkeys trying to snatch the garbage packet. They sure were playful.

Since we had the tea and biscuits we weren't very hungry but since it was lunchtime, we stopped at an Andhra restaurant ( Naam Yaad Nahi 😐 ). The food was good!

We drove back to Bangalore. I also hit the 160 km/hr speed that I wanted to. Yayyy!

The drive, caves, chai, monkeys made our day. This is one place I would recommend to everyone!
