UttariBetta, also known as Huthridurga is a beautiful 5km trail (to and fro), the stunning views of which are hard to forget. At a distance of 75km from Bangalore, it makes for an ideal trek for beginners.
Here's a wiki link for all the curious ones.

Baseline
Crew members: Anmol, Ponkishore, Bitti, Arun, Rishabh, Shubham
Start time: 4:30 AM
Transportation: Ola Drive.

My sister (Bitti) was in town and she also expressed interest in joining me in my trekking expedition. Bitti, Triyansha (cousin) and I, had originally planned for it to be something of a Paarivaarik trek. Triyansha, however, had to drop it because of some urgent official work.
I didn't want just the two of us to go since my driving skills aren't exactly top-notch ðŸ™ˆ. So, I asked my colleagues if they were interested. 4 of them agreed and we were now a group of 6!
Hence, it was Innova Crysta, AGAIN! (We had the same ride, for the Antargange trek as well).



Getting there
Our ride had to be picked up from Vega city mall, Bannergatta at 4 AM. All of us reached by 4:15 and we hit the road by 4:30. The roads were extremely smooth and the weather was also pleasant.
A few minutes from the destination, we stopped, to click a few pictures - with the ride, on the ride :P



We reached the base by 6:30 AM and started the climb.
The trek commences from a tiny village that opens up to fort walls and then goes through seven doors of stone. Heads up for all you tall ones, be careful with your heads!! ðŸ™Š

One of the 7 doors

The trail is mostly rocky - going over boulders and steps carved into huge rocks. I was actually surprised to see none of us going out of breath. It probably was because of the generous climate, the place had to offer.



After 1 and a half-hour of a musical trek, we reached the top.
Parts of the trail were quite steep, and having good shoes definitely helped cover them easily. I assume it would be slightly more challenging when it rains, but not dangerous.
There's one rock formation that reminded us of the Lion King! Um, Picture Toh Banta Tha Boss.




Since I had already made tea the last time, the new folks also wanted to try it out. So yes, the first thing we did was make some chai!

         


Jaipuri Chaiwaale


This time around, we also took some cup noodles along. The tea and the noodles blended well with the view :)

There's a small pond near the Shankareswar temple. It looks peaceful but there are a lot of reptiles underwater. So, I would suggest refraining from going in.


We chilled for a while and then started our descent. Thanks to the clouds, that kept the sun away, we covered the 2.5kms in just 45 mins.

We got back to Bangalore and had our lunch at Jalsa, Banashankari. The food although expensive, was delicious. Deposited the car back and went our ways!


This place is a must!! Especially if you're one for scenic views. :)
