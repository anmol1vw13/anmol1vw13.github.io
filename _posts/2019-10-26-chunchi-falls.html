Chunchi Falls, is one of a kind, day trip for people living in and around Bangalore. It makes for a perfect place for wanderers and photo fanatics.

Located at a distance of approximately 90 km, the water in the falls is sourced from the river Arkavati.

Baseline
Crew members: Anmol, Ponkishore, Amarvir
Start time: 6AM
Transportation: Ola Outstation

Yet another Friday evening with no plans for the weekend. Having been on a trek just last weekend, another one seemed like a stretch.

So I browsed through our good, old and wafaadaar BookMyShow to see if something interesting was taking place :
DJ (Nah)
Movies (Nah)
Standups (Nah)

That's how I made up my mind.....

Of course, since I had convinced myself for this, I assumed, the others would be up for it too. Only Ponkishore agreed but I also managed to convince my flatmate Amarvir on the pretext of finding a place where he could meditate too! (He's quite the yogi, you guys!)
I went through a few blogs and found Chunchi to be the perfect spot. 
Ola outstation was the natural choice of transportation since none of us were confident enough about our driving skills!

Getting there

We started off at 6AM from Bangalore. Midway, I was nauseated and we had to take a break which set us back by about 15mins. We reached at around 9AM. It was a sunny morning. 
There was a stall near the parking spot and since we didn't pack any food, we thought of filling up our bellies, before going down. The food was yum. 3 dosas for Rs. 20. Could it be any cheaper?

The entrance had a few signboards warning about the crocodiles near the falls and had also displayed the count of deaths ( Khoon Bhari Maang? ) Just to make sure if it was safe, we confirmed with the lady at the stall. She said, there aren't any crocodiles anymore, but it still isn't safe to go near the falls.

The breakfast was done and it was time for some skin protection lotion aka sunscreen.
We began our walk and came across a canal bridge. It was exciting to see something like that in the jungle.




We then crossed a watchtower but didn't feel like going up there and after a 10-minute walk down the muddy, rocky terrain, reached the vantage point to witness the fall in all its glory.



We rested up for a while and then saw the prohibition markings on a rock. But being the adventure junkies we are, we decided to ignore it and clicked a picture instead. Amarvir stayed behind for his meditation. 


It was a risky terrain and I wouldn't recommend anyone else to do it. But if you do manage to get through, the spot is simply amazing. The falls, the view, the sound - breathtaking :)
How I wish I had a drone with me! It would have been the perfect gadget.

 
Just for the sake of a picture :P

Although it was just 10 o'clock, it felt like the sun was at its peak. Ponkishore and me, we still spent about an hour and a half chilling, trying to spot them crocodiles. Hahaha. 

I didn't feel like going back, but we had to because Amarvir was alone and the outstation was booked only for a few hours. 

We left the place, came back to Bangalore and had an unusual Kerala cuisine at a place called Coastal Mama in JP Nagar. I don't have pictures to show, but the ambiance was great and the food was delicious. It is a must-try restaurant.

All in all, our day out was worth it!
